"Las Vegas is the only place
I know where money really talks--it says, Goodbye."
--Frank Sinatra

1950's


The New Frontier Hotel was the venue of Elvis Presley's
first shows in Las Vegas from 23 April 1956.


8 million people began flocking to Vegas, per year
beginning in the 50s.


The Gaming Commission of Nevada was established on 30 March 1959.


Sinatra began his long relationship with the Sands in October 1953

Clark County population 48,289. (Las Vegas - 24,624.)

1970's

The MGM Grand Hotel and Casino Opens - World's Largest Hotel


Elvis performs his final engagement at the Hilton.


Las Vegas legend Elvis Presley dies in Memphis.

Clark County population 273,288. (Las Vegas - 125,787.)

1990's


1990: Sammy Davis Jr. dies, followed by fellow Rat Pack members Dean Martin (‘95) and Frank Sinatra (‘98)


The first Hard Rock Hotel opens March 10, 1995


The face of downtown Las Vegas changes forever with the December 14 opening of the $70 million Fremont Street Experience.


Clark County population 741,459. (Las Vegas - 258,295.)


Today

Opening of Slotzilla at Fremont Street Experience. Zip line that stretches the entire length.


Echelon site, formerly known as the Stardust, sold and Resorts World Las Vegas Plans Revealed


Harrah's Entertainment rebrands; becomes Caesars Entertainment


Las Vegas Sets Visitation Record with 42.3 million visitors
